About Gujarat

Gujarat is famous for its vibrant festivals, Garba dance, rich handicrafts, spiritual heritage, and warm hospitality. It reflects simplicity, discipline, and joyful traditions.

Glimpses of Culture

Tourist Locations

Gujarat is known for the Statue of Unity, Gir National Park, Somnath Temple, Dwarka, Rann of Kutch, Sabarmati Ashram, Champaner-Pavagadh Archaeological Park, and Saputara Hill Station.

Statue of Unity

The world's tallest statue at 182 meters, honoring visionary leader Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel.

Rann of Kutch

A mesmerizing salt desert that sparkles white under the moon, famous for its cultural Rann Utsav.

Gir National Park

The exclusive home and sanctuary of the rare Asiatic Lions in their natural forest habitat.

Somnath Temple

A holy shrine on the coast of the Arabian Sea, standing proud as the first among the twelve Jyotirlingas.

Dwarkadhish Temple

A 2200-year-old temple dedicated to Lord Krishna, resting beside the Gomti River in the ancient city of Dwarka.

Sabarmati Ashram

Mahatma Gandhi's peaceful historic residence where the Dandi Salt March originated.

Modhera Sun Temple

An exquisite temple dedicated to the Sun God, displaying outstanding solar architecture and a grand stepped tank.

Folk & Tradition

Gujarat’s folk and traditions are vibrant, joyful, and deeply rooted in community life. Folk dances like **Garba** and **Dandiya Raas** celebrate devotion and togetherness, especially during Navratri. Traditional music, colorful attire, handicrafts, and festivals reflect simplicity, discipline, and cultural pride. Through its customs and celebrations, Gujarat preserves a rich heritage that blends spirituality with everyday life.
