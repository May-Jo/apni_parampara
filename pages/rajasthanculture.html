About Rajasthan

Rajasthan is known for its royal heritage, vibrant traditions, colorful attire, folk dances, majestic forts, and warm hospitality. Every festival, costume, and song reflects the proud history of this desert land.

Did you know?

Capital: Jaipur
Largest state in India by area
Known as the "Land of Kings"
Famous for forts, palaces, folk dances, and camel festivals
Major Language: Hindi and Rajasthani dialects

Glimpses of Culture

Tourist Locations

Rajasthan is famous for Jaipur, Udaipur, Jodhpur, Jaisalmer, Mount Abu, Chittorgarh Fort, Amer Fort, Hawa Mahal, City Palace, and Ranthambore National Park. These places showcase the state's royal heritage and desert beauty.

Hawa Mahal (Jaipur)

The iconic pink sandstone Palace of Winds, featuring 953 intricate windows (jharokhas).

City Palace (Udaipur)

A majestic palace complex on the banks of Lake Pichola, showcasing Mewar architecture.

Mehrangarh Fort (Jodhpur)

A formidable hill fortress rising 410 feet above the Blue City, housing brilliant palaces and museums.

Jaisalmer Desert

Famous Sam sand dunes offering golden camel safaris and overnight camping under a starry sky.

Amer Fort (Jaipur)

A beautiful hilltop fort built of red sandstone and marble, known for its artistic Sheesh Mahal.

Nakki Lake (Mount Abu)

A sacred lake situated in the green Aravalli hills, offering boating and beautiful sunset views.

Ranthambore National Park

Renowned wildlife sanctuary famous for its thriving population of wild Royal Bengal Tigers.

Folk & Tradition

From the graceful Ghoomar dance to soulful folk music, Rajasthan’s culture is deeply rooted in tradition. Festivals like Teej, Gangaur, and Pushkar Fair add life and color to the land.
